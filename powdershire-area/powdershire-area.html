Hensbarrow Beacon

Hensbarrow Beacon is one of Cornwall`s five official Marilyn`s. It has a highest point of 312m, but unusually for a Marilyn it is not the highest point around – indeed it is flanked by the man made waste spoils of the China Clay Industry. However, this makes for an amazing and interesting view (especially as these spoils are being landscaped). As a point of interest, Hensbarrow Beacon is the original Meridian Point for all of the Cornish 1:25 000 maps.

Roundwood Quay – Mush`s Walk

This walk is a lovely circular walk that takes us across fields and alongside creeks and rivers. At its middle point is the historic Roundwood Quay, with evidence of Iron Age importance and an important site to ship out Tin and Copper during the 1800/1900`s. This is a walk close to my families heart, as it was a favourite of my late father-in-laws, hence why I have also called it "Mush's Walk". Rest easy Paul Pennington, always in our thoughts.

Pendower

This coastal walk on the south coast is rather pleasant as it has an inland `loop` and several spots at which to gain a refreshment. It takes in beaches, small headlands and fields and as hills go – not too many of any height. This walk will have given us plenty of opportunity to sit on a beach(or beaches) and just watch the sea. We may even have had the opportunity to chat to those manning the Lookout Station, and even left them a donation to keep this invaluable service running.
